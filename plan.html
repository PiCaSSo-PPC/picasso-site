Project Context & Impact

Global Healthcare Challenge
In 2018, the WHO estimated that 21 million children needed Pediatric Palliative Care (PPC) annually, with Europe and North/Central America accounting for 9% of global needs. As survival rates of children with complex chronic diseases (CCD) increase, so do longer and more expensive hospital stays, often ending in death. Yet, only 5.7% of countries have adequate PPC services.
Portugal's Remarkable Progress
In 2013, Portugal was ranked at PPC provision level 1. By 2018, it had reached level 4, showing significant progress in training and service planning. An estimated 7,828 Portuguese children needed PPC that year. However, access remains limited and referrals delayed. PPC aims to reduce aggressive treatments, improve quality of life, and support families‚Äîbut current care is overly centralized, fragmented, and lacks home support.
Innovation in Assessment
The PaPaS Scale helps identify children aged 1‚Äì19 with CCD and palliative needs for early referral. Its Portuguese version has recently been validated. There is still insufficient data on these patients, especially in fields like pediatric neurology.
Healthcare System Structure
Portuguese hospitals are classified from level I to III, with level III providing the most advanced care. Better coordination between hospitals could improve efficiency and care quality, especially by involving smaller, local hospitals in ongoing support.
Leading the Change
Hospital de Santa Maria is leading a multicenter study to classify PPC needs across Portugal, aiming to improve national PPC organization and develop a care network.

PiCaSSO Digital Platform
PiCaSSO is developing a revolutionary digital platform to improve PPC in Portugal. It will offer a clinical data registry, advanced analytics, and a user-friendly interface for healthcare professionals to track, analyze, and coordinate PPC for children with CCD. The platform will support care planning and network creation through secure, centralized data.
